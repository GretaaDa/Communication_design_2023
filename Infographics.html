A human’s health can be divided into two groups. Firstly, the body’s health and secondly the mental health.

The body’s health more about the physical illness like a cold, flue, headaches, and stomach aches.

The mental health is more about the inner thoughts that the person has. It affects a person's way of thinking, feeling, behaviour or even mood.

Most people think about suicide when they hear mental disorders, but that’s not the only effect of it.

Mental disorder is not like a normal disease where it’s the same for everyone, but one major thing they all have in common is that it’s damaging your body one way or another.

Example eating disorder has an effecting your physical body directly meanwhile depression is heavily tiring your mental state.

One mistake people often do is that they avoid talking about what they are going through and mistreats the people close to them. What we need to do in the society is to normalise talking about our mental health and how to cope with people who need our help.

Society has given the genders stereotypes about what they should talk about. Like the women are allowed to have feelings and should be able to express them meanwhile the men should not talk about how they’re feeling no matter what they’re going through.

There is no specific reason why a person has mental health problems, it usually comes from a wide range of sources, and is individual, on how deeply it affects one's mental state.

But there are some potential factors that can rase some issues for your mental wellbeing:
  • Childhood trauma – abuse, neglect, assault, violence
  • Chronic medical conditions – cancer, diabetes
  • Biological factors – chemical imbalance in the brain, genetics
  • Addiction – drugs, alcohol
  • Social isolation – lowliness, discrimination, poverty, bullying
  • Adult trauma – military, accident, violent crime
  • Physical trauma – head injury, epilepsy
